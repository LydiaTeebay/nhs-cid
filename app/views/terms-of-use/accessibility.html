NHS login - accessibility

We want to make sure the process for setting up an NHS login is easy for everyone who uses it.

Accessibility standards

NHS login has been designed and built to meet the AA standard of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1. There is some content that does not meet this standard, but we are working to fix these problems.

Content that does not meet the standard

Content that does not meet the WCAG 2.1 AA standard includes:

  • when an error message is shown on the 'Do you have an NHS login' question, the message is repeated when a person using a screen reader tries to select one of the options
  • missing page titles from all 'Manage your NHS login' pages
  • a submit button that is wrongly identified as a link on the 'Delete your NHS login' page

Another way to prove who you are

If you cannot take a photo of your I.D. document or record a video, you can prove who you are at your GP surgery instead. You must sign up to your GP surgery's online services, so that you can use your registration details to prove who you are.

You will need these 3 registration details:

  • a Linkage Key (also known as a Passphrase)
  • an O.D.S. Code (also known as an Organisation Code or Practice I.D.)
  • an Account I.D.

Speak to your GP if you have already signed up to your GP surgery's online services but you do not have your registration details.

Last updated
