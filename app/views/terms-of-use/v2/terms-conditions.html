3. Connected services

When you have created your NHS login, it will be valid across the Connected Services you wish to access. We verify your I.D. for the Connected Services you wish to access.

Connected Services will always relate to the provision of NHS or social care services to you. They may be provided by an organisation directly commissioned to provide NHS or social care services, or by a supplier on behalf of a commissioned organisation.

The Connected Services you wish to access will also have their own terms and conditions and privacy notices. Some Connected Services may need you to complete extra compliance before giving you access. This may include extra verification processes. This is outside of our control and responsibility.

We have separate agreements in place with Connected Services to address responsibility for errors in I.D. checking processes. You are responsible for making all arrangements necessary for you to access NHS login. This includes having an internet connection and an appropriate device for access, like a smartphone. We do not guarantee that NHS login will always be available, or that access to it will be error free. We may suspend, stop, remove or change all or any part of NHS login without notice.

For NHS login to work you must use a device, operating system and browser that match our minimum requirements. You can find these minimum requirements on the NHS App help and support page.

If you think that someone knows the username and password to your NHS login, you must quickly reset your password. We have many measures in place to keep your information safe, but it is important that you also play your part - visit the Government's Get Safe Online app for advice on this.

5. Passwordless authentication

Once you have registered to use NHS login you may wish to use passwordless authentication to access your NHS login account and Connected Services. This is the verification of your identity by means of a physical trait, known as biometric data, such as fingerprint and facial recognition. If you do not wish to use passwordless authentication or your device does not support it, then you can still use your existing method, such as a username and password, to access your NHS login account and Connected Services.

We do not have access to, or control over, the biometric data you store on your device. You should make sure that only your own biometric data is stored on your device to prevent anyone else accessing your NHS login and Connected Services accounts. We have the right at any time, without notice, to block the use of passwordless authentication.

If you want to enable or disable passwordless authentication, you can:

  • change the settings of the Connected Service on your device
  • contact the Connected Service
